Hi, I’m Michael

Thanks for visiting my site! I’m a freelancer, currently open to full-time work, based in Los Angeles. I’m originally from Santa Clarita, California, where I lived for the entirety of my childhood. I am the proud recipient of a Master’s in Economics and Social Sciences from Bocconi University, and two Bachelor’s degrees (one in Economics, and a second in Philosophy) from Loyola Marymount University. Within Economics, I have mostly focused on Political Economy and Statistics. My Master’s Thesis explored the role that the internet played in U.S. political polarization between 1996 and 2012, demonstrating heterogeneous treatment effect sizes in different population subgroups through causal forest machine learning. The curious reader can find a copy of that work here.

Since defending my thesis in April of 2020, I have been keeping busy by balancing freelancing and personal projects while looking for steady employment. You can find a link to my GitHub page in the sidebar, where I am currently working on a Shiny app with my friend Mark Barrett. That application tracks performance in a game known as Snappa. Although the game itself is quite non-serious (and exclusively a weekend activity), the project itself has been an enjoyable source of personal growth in coding and data analysis. In the future, I’ll be making write-ups of some of the features that I’ve implemented and the results that I’ve found.

If you would like to get in contact with me, feel free to reach out. My email and other social links are listed in the sidebar, and I’d love to hear from you.
